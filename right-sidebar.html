Dartfish to Analyze

Original 2 second clip of figure skating

In the 2-second video to the left, a figure skater is shown briefly performing a jump on the ice. The video captures their upward movement from the ice, a visible rotation of their body while airborne, and their subsequent return and contact with the ice.


Biomechanical Analysis of a Short Skating Jump

By using and applying the video analysis software on Dartfish, the 2-second video was briefly analyzed. More specifically, body angles throughout the jump, the timing and amplitude of the jump along with landing mechanics were measured using the angle, scale and stopwatch functions of Dartfish. A review on Dartfish

My Experience

The more I worked with Dartfish, the more I came to appreciate its translational potential, both in past applications and in its current relevance for elite athletes. It allows athletes to uncover long-standing flaws in their technique that may have gone unnoticed for years. Once identified, addressing these issues can lead to remarkable improvements in performance and help unlock their full potential.
